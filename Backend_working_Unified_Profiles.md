1. Feature Explanation (Marketing-Friendly)
Unified family profiles let one account make food recommendations that work for the whole household, not just one person. The system combines each family member’s needs (like allergies, eating styles, and health considerations), then suggests meals that are safer, more balanced, and easier for everyone to share.

2. How It Works (Simple)
A user asks for meal ideas (for themselves, a child, or the full family).
The system understands who the request is for: one person, a role in the family (like child), or everyone.
It gathers the right family context and blends it into one practical “household view.”
It checks meal options against family needs so risky or unsuitable options are filtered out.
It applies USDA food pyramid guidance to encourage balance across key food groups.
It returns recommendations and can also show when a plan is missing important food groups.
3. Why It’s Valuable
Helps families avoid meal conflicts caused by different preferences and restrictions.
Reduces decision fatigue by giving “works-for-everyone” suggestions.
Improves safety by accounting for allergies and health-related needs across the household.
Supports healthier day-to-day choices using recognized USDA nutrition standards.
Makes planning faster by creating one coordinated direction instead of separate plans per person.
4. What Makes It Smart
This feature is smart because it uses AI where language is messy and people are vague.
If someone says “plan dinner for the kids tonight,” AI helps understand intent and who the target person is. Then the system uses trusted household rules and USDA guidance to keep recommendations practical and safe.

In plain terms, AI acts like a translator and writing assistant:

It translates natural requests into the right family context.
It helps produce clear, helpful meal guidance.
It does not randomly guess family rules; those are applied consistently by the system.
