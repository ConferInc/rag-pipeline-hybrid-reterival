How the Backend Works:
1. Feature Explanation 
This meal planning feature acts like a nutrition coach that helps build better daily meal choices around your personal needs. It takes your goals, food preferences, allergies, recent meals, and household context, then suggests meal options that are not only personalized but also checked against USDA-style food-balance guidance (protein, dairy, vegetables, fruits, and whole grains). If you ask it to create or change a meal plan, it asks for your confirmation first so nothing is changed by accident.

2. How It Works (Simple)
You ask for meal planning help (for example: “plan meals for next week”).
The system understands what you want and pulls your saved preferences and restrictions.
It looks through your food data in multiple ways to find strong candidate meals.
It removes options that clash with your restrictions (like allergens or certain diet rules).
It favors options that improve balance across USDA food groups, avoid repetition, and better fit calorie goals.
For meal-plan actions, it asks “Should I go ahead?” before triggering any actual update.
It returns clear suggestions (and why they were chosen), plus warnings if food-group coverage is incomplete.
3. Why It’s Valuable
Gives users practical meal options that are personalized, not generic.
Helps users eat more balanced meals using USDA-style food group standards.
Reduces decision fatigue by narrowing choices to better-fit options.
Protects users with dietary/allergen checks before suggestions are shown.
Prevents accidental changes by requiring confirmation for plan creation/updates.
Solves the “I want healthy, realistic meals for me/my family without manual planning” problem.
4. What Makes It Smart
The AI is not just writing random meal ideas. It first gathers real options from your product’s food knowledge, then uses AI to turn that into natural, helpful guidance. It “understands” context like meal timing, preferences, and follow-up questions, and it adapts suggestions based on what you’ve recently eaten so plans stay varied. It also applies USDA-inspired balance checks and can flag when a day’s choices are still missing key food groups, so guidance is both personalized and nutrition-aware.

USDA standards reflected in the feature (in plain terms):

Prioritizes a daily mix of: protein, dairy, vegetables, fruits, and whole grains.
Encourages protein across main meals.
Tries to keep sugar, sodium, and saturated fat within healthier ranges.
Promotes variety and avoids one-sided meal patterns.
